Euler's formula is often called the most beautiful equation in mathematics:
eiπ + 1 = 0

It links five of the most important constants in mathematics: e (the base of natural logarithms), i (the imaginary unit), π (pi), 1, and 0—all in a single, elegant equation. This formula is a bridge between algebra, geometry, and complex analysis, and is celebrated for its simplicity and depth.

As Richard Feynman called it, "the most remarkable formula in mathematics."
